0Dopamine — Eliminate your distractions

The Android app I built to eliminate any distraction, in any app.

2026-07-01
0DopamineAndroid digital wellbeing app · BetaGet it on 0dopamine.com

Why I built it

It all started when YouTube rolled out Shorts. I opened the app to watch a video I'd saved and, out of nowhere, a Short popped up. Then another. And another.

It was like having TikTok wedged inside YouTube — and we all know how TikTok goes: it fries your brain and gets you hooked on a constant dopamine drip. By the time you look up, twenty minutes of mindless scrolling have vanished without you ever deciding to, and whatever you actually opened the app for still isn't done. At first it only happened now and then; before long it was every single time.

And the worst part: there's no way to turn them off. Not even paying for YouTube Premium gets you that option. You get rewarded for staying in the app, not for watching anything good.

So I got the idea to build an app that would wipe out that content. Digging into it, I found that removing it entirely wasn't feasible, but blocking it was. And out of that need, 0Dopamine was born.

At first I made it just for me. Over time I expanded it to block distractions in any app and gave it a polished interface, inspired by the new glass-style design on iOS. And at some point it hit me: I'm clearly not the only one this happens to. So I shipped it.

The goal is simple: I'm not trying to get rich, I'm trying to help as many people as possible. That's why it's private —I don't sell your data or force you to create accounts— and free. You can back it with a donation or, later on, become a sponsor. And once it raises enough to cover the upkeep, I'll pull the ads: then it'll be fully private.

That's 0Dopamine: an Android app made by someone who had the exact same problem you do.
 It's in beta and I make it better with every version.

I'm not asking you to spend zero hours on your phone. Just that you're the one deciding those hours, and not autopilot.

Features: block Reels, Shorts and Stories, but not your messages

Here's the clever bit: you don't have to choose between deleting Instagram and surrendering to the infinite scroll. You block only the addictive part and keep the rest. On Instagram you can cut off Stories and Reels, but you keep calmly reading and replying to messages; on YouTube you kill the Shorts and watch regular videos just fine. Turn some restrictions on, leave others off, or flip them all on: you're the boss here.

What you can block

  • Entire apps, or just their addictive part
  • Instagram: Stories and Reels (messages stay)
  • YouTube Shorts, TikTok and Facebook Reels
  • WhatsApp Statuses, Snapchat Spotlight, videos on X, LinkedIn and Pinterest
  • Mix and match it to fit your day

Five ways to rein yourself in

  • Pause — one breath before you open it, so it's a decision and not a reflex
  • Full block — with a message you write to yourself (get creative)
  • Schedule — the app only opens during the hours you pick
  • Daily limit — a time cap per day, and that's a wrap till tomorrow, champ
  • Mindfulness — 4-7-8 breathing before you go in, for the friend who's into meditation

Real privacy

I don't sell your data. Ever. Everything stays on your device: no mandatory accounts, no servers of mine snooping on what you do. The app uses the Accessibility Service for one thing only: knowing which app you have open in front of you. It doesn't read your messages, your texts, or your passwords. It can't, and even if it could, it wouldn't.

And the ads on the free plan? They're there for one reason only: paying for the servers and keeping this thing alive. Not to make me rich — trust me, this isn't making anyone rich.

Free, and I mean it

I didn't build this to make money, but to actually be useful to people. And to be useful to people it has to be accessible, so 0Dopamine is free. And if you want the full package without spending a cent, all you have to do is share it with someone who needs it too (we all know someone).
